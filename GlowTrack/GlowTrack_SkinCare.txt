Functional Requirements


GlowTrack: Skincare Management & E-Commerce Platform
NAVASCA, SEDRIEL H.
ORLANDA, ARDEE JHADE B.
BSIT-S-2A
1.1 Product Management
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR1.1
	Each skincare product page must present complete information to users, covering the product photo, name, brand, classification (e.g., Cleanser, Moisturizer, Serum), selling price, size or volume, available quantity, applicable skin types, active ingredients, and the name of the seller or brand.
	FR1.2
	Visitors and registered customers must be able to narrow down and reorder product results using criteria such as product type, skin type compatibility, price range, specific ingredients (e.g., Niacinamide, Retinol, Hyaluronic Acid), stock availability, and overall user ratings.
	FR1.3
	Sellers must have the capability to create new product entries, make modifications to existing ones, and deactivate listings as needed, with the ability to update pricing, available stock, descriptions, ingredient details, and product photos.
	FR1.4
	Administrators must be empowered to review and either approve or reject product submissions, feature selected items, or permanently remove any listing from the marketplace.
	FR1.5
	Following the successful completion and delivery of an order, customers must be able to post written reviews, assign ratings, specify their skin type, describe improvements observed, and attach before-and-after photos.
	FR1.6
	Products associated with seller or brand accounts that have undergone and passed administrator verification must display a distinct verified badge on their listing pages.
	

1.2 Sales and Order Management
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR2.1
	A persistent shopping cart must be maintained for authenticated users, enabling them to add new skincare items, modify item quantities, or remove products before proceeding to purchase.
	FR2.2
	The ordering process must guide buyers through selecting a shipping address, entering applicable promo or discount codes, and choosing a preferred payment method, either Cash on Delivery or an online payment option.
	FR2.3
	Once a purchase is confirmed, the platform must automatically generate a unique Order ID and present the buyer with a comprehensive summary of their transaction.
	FR2.4
	Sellers must be able to acknowledge incoming orders, prepare them for shipment, and update the order's progress through various status stages.
	FR2.5
	Administrators must have full visibility into all platform orders and must be able to manually adjust order statuses when handling disputes or escalations.
	FR2.6
	Both buyers and administrators must be permitted to cancel orders that remain in a Pending or Confirmed state, with the system automatically returning the affected quantities back to the product's stock count.
	FR2.7
	Buyers must receive real-time in-app alerts whenever their order advances to a new status, such as when it is confirmed, dispatched, or successfully delivered.
	

1.3 Inventory and Stock Management
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR3.1
	The platform must continuously monitor and record the remaining stock count for every skincare product offered by each registered seller.
	FR3.2
	Inventory quantities must be reduced automatically when an order is confirmed and reinstated whenever an order is cancelled or a product is returned by the customer.
	FR3.3
	Each product's public-facing page must indicate its current availability status, distinguishing between In Stock, Low Stock, and Out of Stock conditions.
	FR3.4
	When a product's remaining quantity falls below a seller-defined minimum threshold, the system must automatically send a low-stock warning to the concerned seller.
	FR3.5
	Sellers must be able to manually record inventory changes, such as incoming stock, damaged items, or expired goods, by providing a reason for the adjustment, with all changes preserved in a historical log.
	FR3.6
	A detailed audit trail must be maintained for each product, recording every inventory modification along with the corresponding date, quantity involved, and the reason for the change.
	

1.4 Skincare Routine Builder
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR4.1
	Registered users must be able to build, save, modify, and remove personalized skincare routines, organized into separate morning (AM) and evening (PM) schedules.
	FR4.2
	Users must be able to assign products from the platform catalog or enter custom product names to each step within their routine, covering steps such as Cleanser, Toner, Serum, Moisturizer, and SPF.
	FR4.3
	When a user's routine includes two or more products containing known incompatible ingredients (e.g., Retinol combined with AHA/BHA in the same step), the system must display a conflict warning to alert the user.
	FR4.4
	Users must be able to mark each routine as completed for a given day, with the system maintaining a visual adherence tracker accessible in both weekly and monthly calendar formats.
	FR4.5
	Users must have the option to make their skincare routines visible to other platform users or restrict them to personal, private access only.
	

1.5 Skin Profile and Progress Tracking
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR5.1
	Users must be able to build and update a personal skin profile that captures their skin type (Oily, Dry, Combination, Sensitive, or Normal), primary skin concerns (such as Acne, Hyperpigmentation, Aging, or Dryness), and any known ingredient allergies or sensitivities.
	FR5.2
	Users must be able to record daily skin journal entries containing the entry date, a condition score from 1 to 5, personal observations, and an optional selfie image to visually document their skin condition.
	FR5.3
	The system must present users with a chronological timeline that brings together their skin condition entries, progress photos, and product usage history in a single, organized view.
	FR5.4
	Based on a user's skin profile and the concerns they have recorded, the system must suggest relevant skincare products tailored to their individual needs.
	FR5.5
	Administrators must be able to add, update, and remove the predefined options for skin types, skin concerns, and ingredient classifications that appear throughout the platform.
	

1.6 Delivery Management
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR6.1
	Administrators must be able to designate a specific delivery personnel to handle a confirmed order, along with setting the anticipated delivery date.
	FR6.2
	Delivery staff must have access to a dedicated list of their active assignments, including the collection point (seller or warehouse address) and the destination address of the buyer.
	FR6.3
	Delivery personnel must be able to progress a delivery through its status stages (Assigned, Picked Up, In Transit, Delivered) and are required to upload a delivery confirmation photo upon completing the handover.
	FR6.4
	Buyers must be able to monitor the live status of their ongoing deliveries directly through the platform at any point during the shipping process.
	FR6.5
	When a delivery is transferred to a different staff member, administrators must be able to perform the reassignment, and both the outgoing and incoming delivery personnel must receive notifications of the change.
	FR6.6
	In cases where a delivery cannot be completed, delivery staff or administrators must be able to flag it as Failed or Returned, which will automatically trigger notifications to both the buyer and relevant administrators.
	

1.7 Customer and Account Management
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR7.1
	Any individual visiting the platform must be able to create a new account by submitting their full name, email address, a secure password, contact number, and home address, with the Buyer role assigned to all newly created accounts by default.
	FR7.2
	Users who wish to sell on the platform must submit their brand or business credentials for review, and they must receive explicit administrator approval before gaining the ability to publish product listings.
	FR7.3
	Registered buyers must have access to a complete record of their past transactions, including the current status of each order, a breakdown of items purchased, and available delivery tracking information.
	FR7.4
	All users, regardless of their role, must be able to modify their account details, including their name, mobile number, delivery address, skin profile information, and profile picture.
	FR7.5
	Administrators must be equipped with tools to search for and access any user account, update account roles or statuses, and apply a soft-delete to accounts when necessary, without permanently erasing the underlying data.
	FR7.6
	Seller accounts must include a publicly accessible brand page that showcases the seller's trade name, full product catalog, promotional visuals, and aggregated ratings gathered from customer reviews.
	

1.8 Reporting and Auditing
ID
	Requirement Description
	FR8.1
	The platform must be capable of producing Sales Reports that present an overview of total earnings, number of units sold, and the average value per order, with filtering options by time period, individual product, brand, or product category.
	FR8.2
	Inventory Reports must be available to display up-to-date stock levels for all products, flag items running low, highlight products approaching or past their expiry date, and provide a complete history of restocking activity.
	FR8.3
	The system must compile Customer Skin Trend Reports that present aggregated platform-wide data on prevalent skin types, the most frequently reported concerns, and the most commonly used ingredients across user profiles and reviews.
	FR8.4
	Seller Performance Reports must be generated to evaluate each seller across key metrics, including total revenue generated, rate of order fulfillment, frequency of returns, and average customer satisfaction rating.
	FR8.5
	All report types available in the platform must support exporting in both PDF and CSV formats to accommodate various documentation and analysis needs.
	FR8.6
	The system must log and store a record of all significant actions carried out by administrators, such as changes to user account statuses, decisions on product approvals, and manual stock adjustments, to support accountability and audit reviews.
